WHAT DO COMPUTER ENGINEERS DO?

Computer engineers work for computer companies such as Intel, HP, and Texas Instruments, and also in industries that build or use computer-based systems, such as telecommunications, automotive, aerospace, etc. Many computer engineers also get jobs as programmers. While they have less programming experience than computer science graduates, their understanding of hardware gives them an advantage in dealing with overall systems.

Hardware Design and Development

Computer engineers design and develop the physical components of computer systems, such as microprocessors, memory modules, circuit boards, and peripheral devices like graphics cards and hard drives.

Software Development

Computer engineers write software programs and develop software systems. They may work on operating systems, device drivers, application software, or embedded software for various devices and platforms.

Computer Architecture

Computer engineers are involved in designing the architecture of computer systems, including selecting the appropriate CPU, memory, and other components to meet specific performance and power requirements.

Embedded Systems

Computer engineers work on embedded systems, which are specialized computer systems integrated into other devices like cars, smartphones, medical devices, and appliances.

Cybersecurity

Computer engineers play a critical role in ensuring the security of computer systems and networks. They work on implementing security protocols, encryption algorithms, and intrusion detection systems to protect against cyber threats.

Robotics

Some computer engineers specialize in robotics, designing and programming robots for various applications, including manufacturing, healthcare, and autonomous vehicles.
